The owners of Christchurch Airport have revealed they’ve spent $42 million to acquire land for a new international airport on the South Island – but face fierce opposition from a 68-year-old homeowner refusing to sell up and make way. The business wants to create a new terminal in Tarras, an hour’s drive from the ski resort town of Wanaka, that would be capable of welcoming planes from Australia. However, Central Otago farmer Philip Parcell, whose 40-hectare cattle farm would need to be demolished, has become the last person on his block to refuse to move. “I have lived here all my life and this is my home,” he told New Zealand publication Stuff. “It’s very unsettling. I don’t want to leave it. The move would be a real wrench. I would prefer to drop dead here.”
